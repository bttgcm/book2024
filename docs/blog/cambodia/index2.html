The Cambodian outlier

visualisation
academia

An exploration on technical and scientific publications versus other development indicators.

Author

Giacomo Butte

Published

May 11, 2024

I have been living and working in Cambodia also in tertiary education. Sometimes when discussing with colleagues on academic partnerships, availability of data, academic publications there is often the impression that Cambodia is an outlier with economic development increasing much faster than academic production. But is that so?

Publication per country

I started to look into datasets that could provide some evidence and I found one by World Bank record of technical and academic publications, one by SCIMAGO and one by Nature.Below how the 3 dataset report data from a set of chosen countries (note publication are in Log-scale).

Correlation with other development indicators

Using a UNDP dataset 1, we can see publications per million how it correlates with other development indices. Positive correlation is found highest in Gross national Income (GNI), particularly in the GNI pro-capita for woman. Sollowing, material footprint (MF) and Human Development Indicator (HDI) with different variants,with the most correlated being the Inequality-adjusted Human Development Index. The first ten parameters by positive correlation end with expectancy at birth. Referring to parameters with negative correlation, only one parameters has a correlation over 0.75 (since negative correlation this means < 0.75): gender inequality index. interesting to notice that both the most positive and negative correlated have a gender component.

1 UNDP (United Nations Development Programme). 2022. Human Development Report 2021/2022: Uncertain Times, Unsettled Lives: Shaping our Future in a Transforming World. New York.

I have been living and working in Cambodia also in tertiary education. Sometimes when discussing with colleagues on academic partnerships, availability of data, academic publications there is often the impression that Cambodia is an outlier with economic development increasing much faster than academic production.
But is that so?

## Publication per country
 I started to look into datasets that could provide some evidence and I found one by World Bank [record of technical and academic publications](https://databank.worldbank.org/reports.aspx?dsid=2&series=IP.JRN.ARTC.SC), one by [SCIMAGO](https://www.scimagojr.com/countryrank.php?order=itp&ord=desc&year=2020) and one by [Nature](https://www.nature.com/nature-index/country-outputs/generate/all/global).Below how the 3 dataset report data from a set of chosen countries (note publication are in Log-scale).
```{r, echo=FALSE, warning=FALSE, message=FALSE}
library(readxl)
library(tidyverse)
library(dplyr)
library(tidyr)
#Population data
pop <- read.csv("population.csv")%>%select("Country.Code", "X2020")
colnames(pop)[colnames(pop) == 'X2020'] <- 'pop'

#Publication data
art <- read.csv("articles.csv")
scimago <- read_xlsx("scimago.xlsx")
scimago$Country.Name<- scimago$Country
nature<-read.csv("nature.csv")
colnames(nature)[colnames(nature) == 'Country.territory'] <- 'Country.Name'

#Join data in one DF
df <- full_join(art,nature, by="Country.Name")%>%full_join(scimago, by="Country.Name")%>%full_join(pop, by="Country.Code")
colnames(df)[colnames(df) == 'Count'] <- 'nature'
colnames(df)[colnames(df) == 'X2020'] <- 'WB'
colnames(df)[colnames(df) == 'Documents'] <- 'scimago'
colnames(df)[colnames(df) == 'Country.Code'] <- 'CC'

#calculate publication per capita
df$WB<- as.numeric(df$WB)
df$WBpp <- df$WB/df$pop*10^6

#Select only relevant columns
df<- df%>%select("Country.Name","CC","WB","nature","scimago","WBpp","Region","pop")

df.tot<- df%>%subset(CC%in%c("TLS","KHM","PHL","IDN","LAO","MMR","THA","NPL","BGD","VNM","MYS"))%>%select("Country.Name","CC","WB","nature","scimago")

df.tot%>%gather(key = "var", value = "value", -CC,-Country.Name) %>%
  group_by(var)%>%ggplot(aes(fill=var, y=log10(value), x=CC)) +
  geom_bar(position='dodge', stat='identity')+scale_color_viridis_d()+theme_bw()+ggtitle("Publications reported in datasets: World bank, Scimago and Nature")
```

## Correlation with other development indicators
Using a UNDP dataset [^1], we can see publications per million how it correlates with other development indices. Positive correlation is found highest in Gross national Income (GNI), particularly in the GNI pro-capita for woman. Sollowing, material footprint (MF) and Human Development Indicator (HDI) with different variants,with the most correlated being the Inequality-adjusted Human Development Index. The first ten parameters by positive correlation end with expectancy at birth.
Referring to parameters with negative correlation, only one parameters has a correlation over 0.75 (since negative correlation this means < 0.75): gender inequality index.
interesting to notice that both the most positive and negative correlated have a gender component.

[^1]:UNDP (United Nations Development Programme). 2022. Human Development Report 2021/2022: Uncertain Times, Unsettled Lives: Shaping our Future in a Transforming World. New York.

## Publications and governance
Using the [Rigorous and impartial public administration index, 1789 to 2023](https://ourworldindata.org/grapher/rigorous-and-impartial-public-administration-index?tab=table), the [State Capacity Index](https://ourworldindata.org/grapher/state-capacity-index?tab=table) and the  [Bertelsmann Transformation Index](https://ourworldindata.org/grapher/basic-state-functions-score-bti).

The first plot shows relation between Bertelsmann Transformation Index [^2] and publication pro-capita.

Rigorous and impartial public administration[^3]

[^3]:Question: Are public officials rigorous and impartial in the performance of their duties? Responses: 0: The law is not respected by public officials. Arbitrary or biased administration of the law is rampant. 1: The law is weakly respected by public officials. Arbitrary or biased administration of the law is widespread. 2: The law is modestly respected by public officials. Arbitrary or biased administration of the law is moderate. 3: The law is mostly respected by public officials. Arbitrary or biased administration of the law is limited. 4: The law is generally fully respected by the public officials. Arbitrary or biased administration of the law is very limited.

[^2]:The Status Index ranks the countries according to the state of their democracy and market economy, while the Governance Index ranks them according to their respective leadership’s performance. Distributed among the dimensions of democracy, market economy and governance, a total of 17 criteria are subdivided into 49 questions.
