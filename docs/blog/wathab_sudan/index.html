Climate prediction and data - Climate prediction can be found at NOAA Climate Prediction Center. Climate Watch has a country profile for Sudan that looks at GHG emission, climate vulnerability and SDGs. An easy way to explore climate data is by using CatchXwith the possibility of downloading basic information on a given catchment in PDF. East Africa Hazard Watch and ThinkHazard offers maps for different types of natural and economic hazards. Natural hazards have been frequent in Sudan, World Bank published data on occurrence and impact.

Crisis analysis ACAPS offers analysis on different crisis. See the one on Sudan 2021 flooding.

Demographics - Opendata for Africa has basic demographic data dis-aggregated by state. For more detailed information, look at the Multiple Indicator Cluster Survey 2014. IOM publishes data on displaced population. Here the page on Sudan. UN population data.

Drought - For maps on risk see Think Hazard, East Africa Hazard Watch , Aqueduct. Sudan has aNational Drought Plan approved in 2018.

Economy - Poverty among Sudanese communities along the eastern borders: A case study from the Kassala and Gedarif States.

Environmental data UNEP has an open data page on Sudan, reports are also available.

UNEP published a Post-Conflict Environmental Assessment that addresses several topics as natural resources, population displacement.

OpenAfrica published datasets on air, water, land and infrastructure

Flooding Regarding flooding, UNITAR published maps on previous floods, NASA has daily updated maps on flooded areas.

People affected in Eastern Sudan can be found on HumData.

GIS data Digital Earth Africa offers useful data and tools on environmental monitoring.

The SDG 6.6.1 Explorer (“Change in the extent of water-related ecosystems over time”) has data and maps on ecosystems. Country based geotif with yearly variation of ecosystems are available.

CMRD GeoPortal is a platform for disseminating open geospatial datasets and maps for the Eastern and Southern Africa region.

Humanitarian response For information regarding humanitarian response, head to this webpage. UNHCR has a dashboard on Sudan. There is also a dashboard on the Ethiopia-Tigray crisis.

Land (use, geology, agriculture) Landuse, geology, agriculturFor general maps on Sudan geology, agricultural areas and hydrology check FAO’s archive. For more recent information, the Humanitarian Data Exchange keeps updates maps on population, settlements, landuse and many more topics. FAO also published a Atlas of Land Use of Sudan divided by state.

WOCAT has a database of soil management project.

Soilgrids visualises several datasets on world soils.

News on the environment On water issues see Water Journalist Africa; for a focus on the Eastern Nile area head to InfoNile.

Code Africa has different datasets worth checking as well as OpenAfrica.

Investigate Africa can be a source as well.

StoryLab Academy offer MOOC course on data and journalism.

Political context - Eastern Sudan ACLED published a report on the political situation and tension in the Horn of Africa.

An analysis on “Tensions at the Sudanese-Ethiopian Border: Conflating New and Pre-Existing Concerns” by the Brussel International Institute; a paper by Malato Wubneh on the Ethio-Sudan border dispute.

The Crisis Group wrote a briefing on the situation on June 24th.

Hear the podcast on the Horn of Africa.

Datasets on conflict can be found at PRIO.

Different publications are available in the Africa Center for Strategic Studies.

Water resources The Water Strategy for Sudan 2021-2031 can be found on the page of the Ministry of irrigation and Water Resources.

FAO Aquastat has a country profile report with an overview of water resources in the country, and AQUAMAPS visualize different datasets.

The Global Surface Water Explorer is another useful source of maps and data.

Water is also cause of conflict, WorldWater published a map about them.

Water related risks for Sudan shown in this country profile.

Water and biodiversity.

Compiled indexes and maps on Water Security, flooding, are available on Aqueduct.

Case studies from Water Alternatives.

Water supply See a summary of finding on this page.

Key resource for the understanding of the Water Supply situation in Sudan is the Sudan Drinking Water Safety Strategic Framework (SDWSSF, 2017).

Sudan Drinking Water Standards

Wind Global Wind Atlas provides maps on wind speed, orography. Similarly, Global Solar Atlas has data on solar irradiation. PVGIS has data on solar panels performance, irradiation useful for PV solar project.
