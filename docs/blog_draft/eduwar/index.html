The 1000+ papers were then uploaded in ASreview and screened for relevance1. At this moment 126 papers out of 1280 were screened.

1 ASReview, developed at Utrecht University, helps scholars and practitioners to get an overview of the most relevant records for their work. After installation, open Command prompt in Windows and run asreview lab.

Snowball

Starting fro one relevant article “How to Maintain Education During Wars? An Integrative Approach to Ensure the Right to Education”. Cited papers and citing papers are extracted. Since the paper is from 2024, no citing work was found.

The same method was used with another paper: Education in Emergencies: A Review of Theory and Research. This produced 84 cited works and 154 citing works.

Removing numbers dfCorpus <- tm_map(dfCorpus, removeNumbers) # 4. Removing punctuation dfCorpus <- tm_map(dfCorpus, removePunctuation) # 5. Removing stop words dfCorpus <- tm_map(dfCorpus, removeWords, stopwords(“english”))

tdm <-TermDocumentMatrix(dfCorpus, control=list(wordLengths=c(1,Inf))) freq <- slam::row_sums(tdm) words <- names(freq)
wordcloud(words, freq, min.freq=4)
